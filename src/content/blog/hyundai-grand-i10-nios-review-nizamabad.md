## Design — Cheerful and Practical

The Grand i10 Nios looks friendly and approachable rather than bold or aggressive. The cascading grille, sharp headlamps, and rounded body work together for a neat appearance.

It is the right size for Nizamabad — compact enough to fit in small parking spots near the old city and markets, yet spacious inside for 4-5 adults. The tall seating position makes entry and exit easy, which elders in the family appreciate.

---

## Interior — Value-Focused

At this price, you do not get luxury — but Hyundai has packed in more than you might expect:

**Infotainment:** 8-inch touchscreen with Android Auto and Apple CarPlay (Magna and above)
**Audio:** 4-speaker system — decent for city use
**Climate control:** Manual AC standard — works efficiently for Telangana heat
**Instrument cluster:** Analogue with digital MID
**Steering:** Tilt adjustable

The plastics feel budget-grade, and there is no sunroof — but the cabin is well laid out, easy to clean (important in dusty Nizamabad summers), and genuinely practical for a family of 4.

---

## Engine and Mileage — The Star of the Show

### 1.2L Petrol
Smooth and reliable. The 83 PS engine is not fast, but it is perfectly adequate for Nizamabad city driving and relaxed highway cruising up to 100 km/h. The 5-speed manual is easy to use.

**ARAI:** 20.7 km/l
**Real-world Nizamabad city:** 16–18 km/l
**Real-world highway:** 19–21 km/l

At ₹104/litre petrol, running costs work out to around **₹5–6 per km** — very economical.

### AMT (Automatic)
The AMT (Automated Manual Transmission) makes the Grand i10 Nios easy to drive in Nizamabad's stop-and-go traffic. Slightly lower mileage than MT but worth it for buyers who want automatic convenience.

### CNG Variant
The CNG variant is the running cost champion. At ₹90/kg for CNG, the 31.59 km/kg mileage means running costs of just **₹2.8–3 per km** — less than half the cost of petrol. Ideal for daily commuters who cover 50+ km/day in Nizamabad.

**Note:** CNG filling station availability in Nizamabad district is improving but not universal — check your regular route before choosing CNG.

---

## Ride Quality — Comfortable for Its Class

For an entry hatchback, the Grand i10 Nios rides surprisingly well. Hyundai has tuned the suspension for Indian roads — it absorbs bumps and small potholes without unsettling occupants.

Highway stability is adequate up to 110 km/h — comfortable for Nizamabad to Hyderabad drives on NH44, though the car does feel light at very high speeds.

The 165mm ground clearance handles normal city roads and speed breakers. Very rough rural roads near Yellareddy or Balkonda require careful driving.

---

## Practicality Assessment

**Boot space (260L):** Adequate for a family's weekend shopping. Not enough for long trips with full luggage — consider the [Hyundai Aura](/en/cars/aura) sedan if you need more boot space.

**Rear seat:** Comfortable for 3 adults on short trips. Two adults with a child in the back is the ideal setup for longer drives.

**Fuel tank (37 litres):** Needs refilling every 600–700 km in highway driving — reasonable for most usage patterns.
