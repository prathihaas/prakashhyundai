## Why Grand i10 Nios Wins in Nizamabad

### 1. Rear AC Vents — The Telangana Summer Advantage

This is perhaps the most practical advantage in the Nizamabad climate. Telangana summers regularly see 43–46°C. Neither the Maruti Swift nor the Tata Tiago has rear AC vents at any price point. The Hyundai Grand i10 Nios Magna+ (approximately ₹6.85L on-road) comes with rear AC vents.

For a family of 4 or 5 in Nizamabad summer — parents in front, children or elderly in the back — rear AC vents are not a luxury. They are a genuine quality-of-life necessity.

### 2. Boot Space and Practicality

| Model | Boot Space | Seat-down boot | Rear legroom |
|-------|-----------|----------------|-------------|
| Grand i10 Nios | 260 litres | ~450L (split fold) | Best in class |
| Maruti Swift | 268 litres | ~450L | Good |
| Tata Tiago | 242 litres | ~390L | Adequate |

The Grand i10 Nios has the best rear legroom in the segment — a crucial advantage for Nizamabad families who regularly fit 5 adults on school trips, wedding travels, or mandal visits.

### 3. Build Quality and NVH

The Grand i10 Nios has noticeably better cabin insulation (NVH — Noise, Vibration, Harshness) than both the Tiago and Swift. On Nizamabad's mix of smooth highways and rough rural roads, the i10 Nios feels more composed and quieter at speed.

The Swift has a sportier feel but cabin insulation is mediocre. The Tiago uses thinner plastics throughout — the dashboard and door panels feel noticeably cheaper compared to the i10 Nios.

### 4. Larger Touchscreen

Grand i10 Nios has an 8-inch touchscreen vs. 7-inch in Swift and Tiago. While this seems minor, the larger display is noticeably easier to use for navigation on Nizamabad–Hyderabad highway drives.
