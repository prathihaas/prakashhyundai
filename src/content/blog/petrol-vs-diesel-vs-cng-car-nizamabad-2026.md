## Petrol — The Default Choice

### Who It's For
Petrol is the right choice for the majority of car buyers in Nizamabad — those who drive **under 1,500 km per month**.

### Advantages
- **Lower purchase price:** Petrol variants are ₹50,000–1.5L cheaper than diesel equivalents
- **Smoother, quieter engine:** Petrol engines are more refined — especially noticeable in premium cars like i20, Verna
- **Maintenance simplicity:** Petrol engines require less specialized maintenance
- **Better for city driving:** Petrol engines are more responsive at low speeds
- **Wider variant choice:** Most Hyundai models offer more petrol variants including entry-level
- **Turbo options available:** Venue, i20, Verna, Creta all offer turbo petrol — performance with good mileage

### Disadvantages
- Higher fuel cost per km than diesel
- Over 1,500–2,000 km/month, running cost difference vs diesel becomes significant

- [Venue](/en/cars/venue) — 17.5 km/l (turbo)
- [Creta](/en/cars/creta) — 17.4 km/l

## Diesel — The Long-Distance Specialist

### Who It's For
Diesel makes financial sense if you drive **more than 1,500–2,000 km per month** OR travel long distances regularly (like Nizamabad–Hyderabad weekly).

### The Mathematics

For Hyundai Venue at 1,500 km/month:

| Fuel | Vehicle | Mileage | Monthly Fuel Cost |
|------|---------|---------|-----------------|
| Petrol (1.0T) | Venue | 14 km/l real | ₹11,100 |
| Diesel | Venue | 20 km/l real | ₹7,050 |
| **Monthly saving with diesel:** | | | **₹4,050** |

Annual saving: **₹48,600**
Diesel premium (purchase): ~₹1.2L

**Break-even point: ~2.5 years at 1,500 km/month**

After break-even, the diesel car costs significantly less to run.

### Advantages
- **Lower running cost** — 20–25% cheaper per km than petrol in real-world
- **More torque** — effortless highway cruising, easy overtaking on NH44
- **Better mileage** — particularly on highway runs

### Disadvantages
- **Higher purchase price:** ₹1–1.5L premium over petrol equivalent
- **Slightly noisier at idle:** Modern Hyundai diesel engines are much improved, but still noisier than petrol at idle
- **Slower to warm up:** Less efficient for very short trips under 5 km
- **Maintenance costs slightly higher:** Diesel particulate filter on newer engines

### Recommended Diesel Models at Prakash Hyundai
- [Venue](/en/cars/venue) 1.5L diesel — 23.4 km/l ARAI (outstanding)
- [Creta](/en/cars/creta) 1.5L diesel — 21.8 km/l ARAI
- [Alcazar](/en/cars/alcazar) 1.5L diesel — 20.4 km/l ARAI

## CNG — The Running Cost Champion

### Who It's For
CNG is best for buyers with **very high daily mileage (2,000+ km/month)** who commute daily within Nizamabad city and can access a CNG filling station on their regular route.

### The Mathematics

For Grand i10 Nios CNG at 2,000 km/month:

| Fuel | Vehicle | Mileage | Monthly Fuel Cost |
|------|---------|---------|-----------------|
| Petrol | Nios | 16 km/l real | ₹13,000 |
| CNG | Nios CNG | 27 km/kg | ₹6,800 |
| **Monthly saving with CNG:** | | | **₹6,200** |

Annual saving: **₹74,400** — extraordinary savings for daily commuters.

### Advantages
- **Lowest running cost** — approximately ₹2.8–3.5/km vs ₹6–7/km for petrol
- **Environmentally cleaner** — lower CO2 emissions than petrol or diesel
- **Lower purchase premium** vs petrol (only ₹70,000–1L premium for CNG variant)

### Disadvantages
- **Boot space compromised:** CNG cylinder occupies boot space in factory-fitted CNG
- **Slightly less power:** CNG variants have 10–15 PS less power than petrol equivalents
- **CNG filling station dependence:** Must be on your regular route — check availability
- **Longer refuelling time:** CNG takes 5–10 minutes vs 3 minutes for petrol/diesel

### CNG Availability in Nizamabad
CNG infrastructure in Nizamabad is growing. As of 2026, CNG stations are available at select locations in Nizamabad town. Before buying CNG, confirm that a CNG station is conveniently located on your daily route.
