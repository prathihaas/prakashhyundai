## What Goes Into Maintenance Costs

Annual car maintenance includes:
1. **Periodic service** (every 10,000 km or 1 year): oil change, filter replacements
2. **Wear and tear parts**: tyres, brake pads, battery (over 2–5 years)
3. **Occasional repairs**: belts, suspension components, AC gas refill
4. **Insurance renewal**: annual cost
5. **Insurance claims**: deductible on damages

We focus on items 1–3 (regular running costs excluding insurance) for our comparison.

## Tips to Reduce Maintenance Costs

### 1. Follow Service Intervals Precisely
Delaying service increases engine wear and can cause costly failures. Stick to Hyundai's recommended 10,000 km or 12-month intervals.

### 2. Use Genuine Parts
Cheaper aftermarket parts may cost less initially but often fail sooner and can damage other components. At Prakash Hyundai, only Hyundai-genuine parts are used.

### 3. Check Tyre Pressure Monthly
Correct tyre pressure extends tyre life significantly. Under-inflated tyres wear 20–30% faster. Free tyre pressure check at Prakash Hyundai with any service.

### 4. Use the Right Grade Engine Oil
Hyundai specifies particular oil grades for each engine. Using the correct grade maintains fuel efficiency and engine longevity. We use Hyundai-recommended oils in all services.

### 5. Buy Extended Warranty
Hyundai's extended warranty (HEW) covers unexpected repair costs up to 5 years. For peace of mind, the premium is often worth it.
