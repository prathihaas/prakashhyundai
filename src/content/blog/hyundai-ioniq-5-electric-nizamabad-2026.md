## The Ioniq 5 — What Makes It Special

### Ultra-Modern Design
The Ioniq 5 looks like a concept car brought to life. Inspired by the 1970s Hyundai Pony, its pixelated design language, flush door handles, and retro-futuristic proportions are unlike any other car. In Nizamabad, it will be the most distinctive vehicle on any road.

### Vehicle-to-Load (V2L) Technology
The Ioniq 5 can power external devices — phones, laptops, even appliances — from the car's battery. This feature is unique in India and genuinely useful during Nizamabad's occasional power outages.

### Ultra-Fast Charging
With a 350kW DC charger, the Ioniq 5 charges from 10% to 80% in approximately 18 minutes. This is faster than any other EV available in India.

## Range — Will It Reach Hyderabad?

WLTP range is 631 km. Real-world range in Indian conditions (climate control on, highways and city mixed) is typically **450–500 km**.

**Nizamabad to Hyderabad (NH44) distance: ~148 km**

On a full charge, the Ioniq 5 can do **Nizamabad–Hyderabad and back (296 km total)** with significant range to spare — even in hot weather.

For purely local Nizamabad use (city driving + district travel to Bodhan, Kamareddy, Armoor), a single charge lasts **multiple days** for most users.

## Charging Infrastructure — The Honest Picture

This is the most important question for Nizamabad buyers.

### Home Charging (Primary Method)
A **11 kW home charger (AC)** installed at your home overnight charges the Ioniq 5 from empty to full in about 7 hours. If you charge every night, you always start the day with a full battery.

**Home charging cost in Nizamabad:** Telangana residential electricity rate is approximately ₹5–7/unit. Charging 72.6 kWh battery costs approximately ₹360–510 for a full charge.
**Running cost: ₹0.7–1.1 per km** — vs ₹6–7/km for a petrol SUV at current prices.

Home charging works perfectly for most Nizamabad buyers who drive within the city and district.

### Public Fast Charging — Current Status
As of 2026, the public EV charging infrastructure in Nizamabad is developing. The Telangana government has been expanding TSREDCO and private EV charging networks.

**Key charging locations near Nizamabad:**
- Hyderabad has extensive fast charging (Statiq, ChargeZone, Tata Power network)
- NH44 has a growing number of fast chargers
- Nizamabad city charging infrastructure is present but limited

**For long trips beyond district:** Plan around charging stops, though the 450km+ real-world range means most common routes require at most one charge.
