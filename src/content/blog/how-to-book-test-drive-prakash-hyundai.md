## Why Every Car Buyer Should Test Drive

Before we get into the how, here is the why:

**What you discover on a test drive that you cannot learn online:**
- How the seat fits your body (height, legroom, reach to pedals)
- Whether rear passengers in your family are comfortable
- How the AC performs in Nizamabad's heat
- Engine noise levels at city speeds and highway speeds
- How the car handles Nizamabad's speed breakers
- Whether the infotainment system is intuitive for you
- How the clutch/gearbox feels (especially important for AMT/DCT owners)
- Whether you can see clearly through all windows

Many buyers who test drive a car they initially ruled out end up buying it — and many who were sure about a choice change their mind after sitting in alternatives.

## Preparing for Your Test Drive — Checklist

Before the test drive, prepare these questions:

### About Features
- [ ] Which variant am I testing? Does it have the features I need?
- [ ] How does the infotainment system work?
- [ ] Where are the AC controls for rear passengers?
- [ ] How does the sunroof (if applicable) operate?
- [ ] How does the parking camera/sensors work?

### About Practicality
- [ ] Can my family sit comfortably (sit in the rear seat yourself)?
- [ ] Does the boot fit my typical luggage?
- [ ] Can elders in my family enter and exit easily?
- [ ] Is the driving position comfortable for my height?

### About Finance
- [ ] What is the on-road price of this variant?
- [ ] What are the EMI options?
- [ ] What is included in the standard warranty?

## How to Conduct an Effective Test Drive

Ask for at least **20–30 minutes** of driving time. Here is what to cover:

### In the Showroom (5 minutes before driving)
1. **Adjust the seat and mirrors** — make sure you are comfortable
2. **Check all controls** — where is everything? Is it intuitive?
3. **Sit in the rear seat** — how much legroom and headroom? Can 3 adults fit?
4. **Check the boot** — is it big enough?

### City Section (first 10 minutes)
Cover a section of Nizamabad city traffic. Check:
- Clutch/gear shifting feel (MT) or gearbox smoothness (AT/DCT/AMT)
- Visibility in traffic
- How the car handles a speed breaker at 20–30 km/h
- Turning radius — can you maneuver in tight spaces?
- AC cooling speed — does it cool the cabin quickly?

### Highway Section (if possible)
Take the car onto a main road at 60–80 km/h:
- Is the car stable at highway speeds?
- Can you hear the engine noise at 80 km/h?
- How does the car feel during overtaking?
- Does the suspension feel firm or plush on smooth highway?

### Specific to Your Concern
If you have rough roads near your home or office, specifically ask to drive on a section of rough road.
