## Why Hyundai Creta Electric Wins in Nizamabad

### 1. Higher Real-World Range — Critical for North Telangana

The Nizamabad–Hyderabad route is 180km. A round trip is 360km. This is the key test for an EV in this region.

**Nexon EV (40.5 kWh):** Real-world range approximately 280–320 km. A round Nizamabad–Hyderabad trip is tight — you'd need a charge in Hyderabad or face range anxiety.

**Creta Electric (51.4 kWh):** Real-world range approximately 340–380 km. Nizamabad–Hyderabad–Nizamabad on one charge is comfortable, with 20–40 km buffer.

For Nizamabad buyers considering an EV for occasional Hyderabad runs, Creta Electric is the only practical option.

### 2. Faster AC Charging

Hyundai Creta Electric supports 11 kW AC charging (vs. Nexon EV's 7.2 kW). With a home 7.4 kW charger:
- Creta Electric: 0–100% in approximately 6.5 hours (overnight ready)
- Nexon EV: 0–100% in approximately 8.5 hours (tight overnight schedule)

### 3. V2L — Vehicle to Load

Hyundai Creta Electric supports Vehicle to Load (V2L) — you can power home appliances, a laptop, or emergency devices from the car's battery. In North Telangana, where power cuts still occur, this is a genuinely useful feature.

Nexon EV does not offer V2L.

### 4. ADAS — Available More Broadly

Nexon EV ADAS is limited to the Empowered+ top variant. Creta Electric's ADAS is available from the Executive variant upward (₹19.49L on-road area), giving more buyers access to safety tech.

### 5. Premium Interior and Larger Boot

Creta Electric's cabin is noticeably more premium than Nexon EV's. The 433-litre boot (vs Nexon's 350L) is more practical for long-distance travel with luggage.
