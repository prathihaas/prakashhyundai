## The Truth About Toyota Glanza

**The Toyota Glanza is a rebadged Maruti Baleno.** This is not a criticism — it is a fact. Toyota and Maruti have a badge-engineering partnership, and the Glanza is built at Maruti's Manesar plant on the Heartect platform with Toyota badges applied.

What this means for Nizamabad buyers:
- You are buying Maruti quality at Toyota prices (Glanza base ₹6.73L vs Baleno ₹6.61L — ₹12,000 premium for the Toyota badge)
- Toyota service rates are higher than Maruti for what is essentially the same car
- The Glanza has no unique features vs. the Baleno at the same price point
- **It does NOT have ADAS. It does NOT have a sunroof in base/mid variants.**

If you are buying a Toyota for Toyota quality, the Glanza is not the right way to get it.

### 1. Sunroof Available from Mid Variant

Maruti Baleno sunroof is only available on ZXi+ (₹9.5L+). Toyota Glanza has no sunroof option. Hyundai i20 Sportz 1.2 petrol at **₹9.27 lakh** comes with a sunroof.

Hyundai i20 SX 1.2 variant offers ADAS safety features that neither Baleno nor Glanza have at any price:

For a family car on NH44 or in Nizamabad city traffic, these safety features are genuinely valuable.

### 3. Rear AC Vents

Baleno and Glanza have no rear AC vents. In Telangana summers (45°C), this matters enormously. Hyundai i20 has rear AC vents across variants — making it a much more comfortable car for back-seat passengers, especially children.

### 4. BlueLink Connected Car vs Suzuki Connect

Hyundai BlueLink offers more features than Suzuki Connect — including remote engine start, live tracking, SOS emergency calls, and more comprehensive smartphone integration.

### 5. Overall Premium Feel

The i20's cabin feels genuinely premium — soft-touch materials, better NVH insulation, and a more mature dashboard design. The Baleno's cabin is functional but not premium. The Glanza's cabin is identical to the Baleno's.
