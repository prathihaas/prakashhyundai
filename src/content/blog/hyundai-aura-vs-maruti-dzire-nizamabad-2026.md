# Hyundai Aura vs Maruti Dzire 2026 — Nizamabad Comparison

When a Nizamabad buyer wants a compact sedan (car with a separate boot/dicky), two names dominate the shortlist: the [Hyundai Aura](/en/cars/aura) and the Maruti Suzuki Dzire. Both are well-proven, practical, and popular in the ₹6.5L–₹10L range.

This is a head-to-head comparison to help you decide.

## Price — Aura Starts Cheaper

The Aura starts ₹30,000 cheaper than the Dzire. At entry level, this is meaningful for budget-conscious buyers in Nizamabad. The Dzire tops out at ₹10.04L vs Aura at ₹9.37L.

**Advantage: Slight Aura** (lower entry price across the range)

---

## Mileage — Dzire's Clear Advantage

This is the most significant difference in this comparison. The Maruti Dzire's newer Z-series engine achieves a remarkable 24.12 km/l ARAI — among the highest in the compact sedan segment.

The Hyundai Aura achieves 20.6 km/l — still excellent, but noticeably less than the Dzire.

**Real-world Nizamabad:**
- Dzire: 19–21 km/l city
- Aura: 16–18 km/l city

For a driver covering 1,500 km/month, the Dzire saves approximately **₹1,200–1,500/month** in fuel. Over 5 years: **₹72,000–90,000 savings**.

### CNG Mileage — Even More Dramatic

Dzire CNG: 33.73 km/kg
Aura CNG: 25.4 km/kg

The Dzire CNG's mileage is extraordinary. For daily commuters in Nizamabad who can access CNG, the Dzire's fuel economy is near-unbeatable.

**Advantage: Dzire** (significantly better fuel economy in both petrol and CNG)

---

## Boot Space — Aura Wins

The Aura's 402L boot is 20 litres larger than the Dzire's 382L. Both are practically excellent for a compact sedan — you can fit 3–4 large suitcases comfortably.

The Aura's boot opening is also slightly wider, making loading easier.

**Advantage: Aura** (larger, more accessible boot)

---

## Interior Quality and Features

**Aura interior:** Modern, clean design. The 8-inch touchscreen (from Magna variant) is intuitive and responsive. Cabin quality is similar to the Grand i10 Nios interior.

**Dzire interior:** The latest Dzire has improved significantly — the interior feels fresh and well-organized. The 9-inch touchscreen in higher variants is slightly larger than Aura's 8-inch.

Both offer Android Auto and Apple CarPlay on mid-to-top variants. The Dzire's Suzuki Connect and Aura's Android Auto are broadly comparable.

**Advantage: Marginal Dzire** (slightly larger screen in top variants)

---

## Ride Comfort

Both compact sedans ride well for their class. The Aura inherits the Grand i10 Nios's comfortable suspension tuning. The Dzire's recent generation has also improved ride quality significantly.

For Nizamabad's mix of good and broken roads, both handle adequately. The Aura edges slightly ahead in pure ride comfort.

**Advantage: Marginal Aura** (slightly more comfortable suspension)

---

## CNG Availability — Important for Nizamabad Buyers

If you are considering CNG variants, check CNG station availability on your regular route in Nizamabad before deciding. Both cars are well-suited for CNG use, but the Dzire's 33.73 km/kg CNG mileage makes the financial case even stronger if CNG is accessible.

## Verdict

**For mileage-conscious buyers:** Maruti Dzire — the fuel economy advantage is substantial and the resale value is excellent.

**For buyers who want local authorized service and a larger boot:** Hyundai Aura — Prakash Hyundai's Nizamabad service centre and the 402L boot are genuine advantages.

**For CNG users:** Dzire has the superior mileage — 33.73 km/kg is outstanding.

Overall, the Dzire's combination of fuel economy and resale value makes it the more logical choice for most compact sedan buyers on pure numbers. However, the Aura is the smarter choice for buyers who prioritize local Hyundai service and a slightly larger boot.
