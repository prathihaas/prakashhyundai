# Hyundai Exter Review — Best Micro-SUV for Nizamabad 2026?

The micro-SUV segment is one of the fastest growing in India, and Hyundai joined it with the [Exter](/en/cars/exter). Starting at just ₹6.13 lakh, the Exter gives you genuine SUV styling, decent ground clearance, and modern features at a price that overlaps with premium hatchbacks.

For Nizamabad buyers who want the SUV look and feel without the SUV price tag, the Exter is a very compelling option.

---

## What Makes the Exter Special?

The Exter fills a gap in the market: it gives you **SUV ground clearance (185mm) and styling at a hatchback price (₹6–10L)**. Previously, buyers had to choose between affordable hatchbacks with low clearance or expensive SUVs with proper clearance.

The Exter is also notable for having **more boot space (391L) than the Venue (350L)** — remarkable for a smaller car.

---

## Design — Genuinely SUV-Like

The Exter looks properly like a mini-SUV:
- H-shaped DRL signature at the front
- Chunky wheel arch cladding
- Silver skid plates front and rear
- Tall boxy profile maximizing interior space
- 15-inch alloy wheels on higher variants

The design is youthful and energetic — it appeals strongly to buyers in their 20s and 30s who want something different from a conventional hatchback. In Nizamabad's streets, the Exter stands out.

---

## Interior — Feature-Rich for the Price

The Exter's interior punches well above its price:

- **8-inch touchscreen** with wireless Android Auto/Apple CarPlay (Magna and above)
- **Sunroof** from S variant — the only car in this price range with a sunroof
- **Voice-enabled BlueLink** connected car features (top variants)
- **Wireless charging** (Sx variant)
- **6 airbags** (top variants)
- Rear camera standard on all variants

The cabin quality is better than the Grand i10 Nios — slightly more premium plastics and a more modern dashboard design. The seating position is higher, giving a commanding view of the road.

---

## Performance and Mileage

### 1.2L Petrol
The same proven 83 PS engine from the Grand i10 Nios and i20. Adequate for Nizamabad city use and relaxed highway cruising.

**ARAI:** 19.4 km/l
**Real-world Nizamabad:** 15–17 km/l city, 18–20 km/l highway

### AMT (Automatic)
Easy in city traffic. The AMT is slightly jerky at very low speeds but manageable. For buyers who want an automatic under ₹9 lakh, the Exter AMT is a good choice.

### CNG Variant
Excellent running economy at 27.1 km/kg. Lower than the Nios CNG but still very economical. Good for high-mileage daily commuters.

---

## Ground Clearance — The Key Differentiator

At 185mm, the Exter's ground clearance is significantly higher than:
- Hyundai i20 (161mm)
- Maruti Baleno (163mm)
- Tata Altroz (165mm)

This makes the Exter noticeably more capable on:
- Rough approach roads to Nizamabad's older neighbourhoods
- Village roads near Yellareddy and Balkonda mandals
- Monsoon season when roads develop potholes

For buyers in semi-urban Nizamabad or those who visit rural areas frequently, this matters.

## Exter vs Tata Punch — Key Comparison

| Factor | Hyundai Exter | Tata Punch |
|--------|--------------|-----------|
| Starting Price | ₹6.13L | ₹6.13L |
| Top Price | ₹10.3L | ₹10.16L |
| Ground Clearance | 185mm | 187mm |
| Boot Space | 391L | 366L |
| Safety Rating | 3-star GNCAP | 5-star GNCAP |
| Sunroof | Yes (Sx) | Yes (select) |
| BlueLink | Yes | No |
| AMT available | Yes | Yes |

**Safety note:** The Tata Punch has a 5-star GNCAP rating vs the Exter's 3-star. Safety-conscious buyers should consider this carefully. However, the Exter's higher variants include 6 airbags which significantly improves protection.

**Our view:** The Exter offers better connectivity features (BlueLink), more boot space, and broader variant range. The Punch has better crash test ratings. For buyers prioritizing safety above all, the Punch edges ahead. For features and connectivity, the Exter wins.

## Pros and Cons

### Pros
- SUV styling and ground clearance at hatchback prices
- 391L boot — more than Venue
- Sunroof available under ₹9 lakh
- BlueLink connected car features
- CNG option for economical running
- AMT automatic available for city convenience
- Good resale value

### Cons
- 3-star GNCAP safety rating (Tata Punch has 5-star)
- The 1.2L engine is not powerful for highway overtaking
- AMT gearbox is not as smooth as DCT
- No diesel option
